The same loops that keep players grinding keep customers subscribing and congregations tithing. This atlas traces 49 engagement mechanics across games, business, and belief — what fires in the brain, what binds the group, where each one earns loyalty, and where it curdles into a dark pattern.

Colophon & caution

Each mechanic is charted on two axes: me ⟷ we (does it work on a private mind or on a group?) and carrot ⟷ stick (does it pull with desire or push with fear of loss?). Coordinates are editorial judgments, not measurements — argue with them, that's the point.

Hazard marks matter. Most of these mechanics are dual-use: the loop that builds a habit can build a dependency, and the ritual that builds a community can build a cult. The strongest consumer companies borrow from casinos and churches alike — the durable ones are honest about which shelf they're borrowing from.

Examples span games, business, religion & high-control groups, and the wider world (military, fitness, politics). Sightings marked ▲ worked, ▼ backfired or crossed a line, ◆ contested.

Built as a single self-contained HTML file — no external scripts, fonts, tracking or network calls. Deep-link any mechanic by opening its dossier and copying the URL.
